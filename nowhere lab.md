principles
- the actor does not “want to do it,” but “refuses inaction (non-doing)
- constancy of search, systematic renewal of points of view and tireless movement forward in polemics with oneself
- transformation from one image to another, from character to character in front of the audience exclusively by means of his craft, that is, “poorly”
- when no one is sure of anything and nothing is obvious to anyone, then the necessary field of confidence, where only barriers can be overcome, remains the tangibility, concreteness and corporeality of the organism. Only a myth embodied in an actor, in his living organism, can function as a taboo. The violation of the secrecy of a living organism, its revelation in physiological concreteness and internal impulses, going so far that it overcomes even the barriers of excess, returns the mythological situation to its universal human concreteness and becomes the comprehension of truth.
