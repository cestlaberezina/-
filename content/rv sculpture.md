Two sculptures made from wild Rioni Valley soil embody the tension between destruction and resilience. Glazed and fired at 1050 °C, they were damaged during production and rebuilt with raw soil, reflecting both the hydroelectric dam’s disruption and the valley’s enduring strength. Tbilisi, 2020.
