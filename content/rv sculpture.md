the artist works with [[soil]] from the riverbank of the rioni valley, lechkhumi, a [[place]] that has become a center of [[resist]]ance against [[environment]]al destruction in recent years. the ongoing construction of a hydroelectric dam, supported by the [[government]], has already disrupted the lives of the valley’s in[[habit]]ants. some [[fam]]ilies have been forced to leave their [[home]]s due to the [[threat]] of [[flood]]ing, while others remain concerned about the impact on agriculture and their livelihoods. (2020)

two sculptures created from this soil [[embod]] the [[tension]] between destruction and [[resilience]]. crafted from [[wild]] rioni soil, glazed and fired at 1050℃, they were [[damage]]d during the [[process]] and then rebuilt using raw rioni soil, [[symbol]]izing both the disruption caused by the dam project and the resilience of the valley’s people.
