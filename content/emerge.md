[[tbilisi]], december 22, 2021 – on the [[winter]] [[solstice]], a [[moment]] suspended [[between]] [[day]] and [[night]], the [[soul]]'s [[quiet]] [[contemplat]]ion [[become]]s an [[act]]. the text of [[bardo thodol]], often referred to as the tibetan book of the dead, [[offer]]s a recurring theme of [[light]] — an awareness that teeters on the [[threshold]] between [[death]] and re[[birth]], between [[chao]]s and [[trans]]cendence. the light is always near, but elusive, as if [[exist]]ing at arm’s length. this [[liminal]]ity mirrors our deepest human [[experience]]s, where we constantly strive toward something [[beyond]] us, yet never quite [[grasp]] it.

the artist, sensitive to hope and despair, in[[habit]]s this space in between. the work reflects on almost aquatic [[sensation]] — like a [[fish]] [[submerg]]ed, feeling the light [[above]] but un[[able]] to [[touch]] it. this image of being [[surround]]ed by an unknown yet [[comfort]]ing light is not only visual; it becomes an [[emotion]]al and [[spirit]]ual [[encounter]]. there’s a quiet [[hope]], a [[tender]]ness.

on this particular solstice morning, pauline rose before [[dawn]], [[step]]ping out of the [[mountain]] house where she had been living that winter. despite the [[absence]] of [[snow]] in the beginning, later a first snowflake fell and the [[landscape]] became a canvas. the solstice, a time of renewal and reflection, [[invit]]es us to [[pause]] and [[observ]]e the quiet [[mirac]]les that [[exist]] around us.
