This series was created using raw, unprocessed red clay sourced from a village in Upper Ajara, Georgia. The artist explored simple forms and primitive techniques, embracing the unpredictability of firing and the material’s natural qualities. White glaze derived from the same clay fragments was applied by hand to extend the work’s expressive range, while intentional hollows in the vessels highlight absence and the viewer’s role in completing the piece. Five vessels, unprocessed Brili clay, glaze, fired at 1050℃. Exhibited at [[earthern]]. Tbilisi, Georgia, 2021
