in this work, pauline explores the [[act]] of [[touch]] as both a physical and [[philosoph]]ical encounter. through the [[tactil]]e [[process]] of [[sculpt]]ing, the artist [[question]]s who is touching and who is [[receiv]]ing, pondering the [[space]] [[between]] them, the [[depth]] of the touch, and what [[remain]]s after it. the act of touch becomes a [[dynamic]] [[exchange]], one that [[trans]]cends the [[surface]] of the [[material]].

for the artist, clay is not simply a [[medium]], but a [[body]] in itself. the [[sensation]] of working with it is akin to massaging it, with the artist’s [[attent]]ion [[shift]]ing away from the surface to the internal dynamics — the points of [[tension]], [[void]]s, and centers that reveal themselves as the process [[unfold]]s. there is an inherent [[resist]]ance in the material, sometimes stronger than the artist's [[intent]]ions, creating a [[dialogue]] where the artist is not the sole actor but a [[listen]]er and follower, [[yield]]ing to the clay’s own will.

this conversation between the artist and the material raises questions about [[presence]] and [[absence]], touch and [[perception]], and the [[boundar]]ies of physical interaction. the resulting sculptures are not simply [[object]]s but embody the exchange, the tension, and the quiet [[surrender]] to the material’s own [[agency]].

two sculptures, unprocessed brili clay, glaze, fired at 1050℃. exhibited at [[earthern]]
