In this work, pauline explores the [[act]] of [[touch]] as both a physical and [[philosoph]]ical encounter. Through the [[tactil]]e [[process]] of [[sculpt]]ing, the artist [[question]]s who is touching and who is [[receiv]]ing, pondering the [[space]] [[between]] them, the [[depth]] of the touch, and what [[remain]]s after it. the act of touch becomes a [[dynamic]] [[exchange]], one that [[trans]]cends the [[surface]] of the [[material]].

The artist’s [[attent]]ion [[shift]]ing away from the surface to the internal dynamics — the points of [[tension]], [[void]]s, and centers that reveal themselves as the process [[unfold]]s. there is an inherent [[resist]]ance in the material, sometimes stronger than the artist's [[intent]]ions, creating a [[dialogue]] where the artist is not the sole actor but a [[listen]]er and follower, [[yield]]ing to the clay’s own will. Two sculptures, unprocessed brili clay, glaze, fired at 1050℃. Exhibited at [[earthern]]. Tbilisi, Georgia, 2021.
