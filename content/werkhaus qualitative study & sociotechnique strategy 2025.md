“Werkhaus Qualitative Study & Sociotechnique Strategy,” developed by Pauline together with [Antoine Depreux](https://www.instagram.com/antoine_depreux/) , examined the internal [[dynamic]]s of one of Nowhere Festival’s oldest and most engaged camps. The study focused on [[commitment]], members’ [[capacity]] for [[real]]-life [[involve]]ment, motivation, contradictions within the festival’s [[ethics]], camp-building logistics, [[politic]]al [[belief]]s, [[value]]s, and the personal, social, political, and physical pressures shaping participation. It also addressed preferences for self-organisation ([[together with]]), attitudes toward [[hierarchy]] and bullying, decision-making models, criteria for joining the camp and the practical [[limit]]s of co-lead authority.

The results of the survey were anonymised, presented and discussed with the camp members pre-event.

The project functioned as a social and political [[experiment]] within a 20-year-old festival [[structure]]. Once the camp chose blind voting and majority rule, Pauline and Antoine stepped down, as this model [[conflict]]ed with their political stance and made their presence unnecessary. By that point their work was complete. The camp operated successfully during the festival, with leadership roles distributed voluntarily among members; for the last 10 years the camp was handled by just one person.
