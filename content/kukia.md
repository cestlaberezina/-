In june 2022, pauline acquired a [[modest]], aging house from a [[friend]] — an [[abandon]]ed [[space]] brimming with [[potential]] yet in [[need]] of significant [[work]]. What [[follow]]ed was a five-month [[transformation]], blending [[practic]]al construction with artistic [[vision]]. With a limited budget and the help of friends and workers, the artist set out to reconstruct the house, turning it into a livable, [[dynamic]] space. This endeavor wasn’t just about making a house [[function]]al — it was an act of creation, a reimagining of what a [[home]] could be.

A skylight was carved out to [[flood]] the space with [[natur]]al [[light]], while the old roof was carefully [[repair]]ed to [[exten]]d the house’s lifespan. Walls were built, others destroyed, and the flooring was cleaned from [[layer]]s of [[plast]]ic.

The artist also designed and sculpted essential [[element]]s, such as the bath, toilet, and kitchen table. These functional pieces were crafted with a level of [[thought]]fulness, blending [[utilit]]y with form. The kitchen table, for example, wasn’t just a piece of furniture but a statement of the space’s creative ethos.

In keeping with the [[desire]] for sustainability, the artist also incorporated natural elements, creating a [[moss vertical garden]] that would serve as both a decorative and grounding feature of the home. On a more [[architectur]]al note, the artist built a second floor and reinforced the roof’s structure with careful construction, ensuring the house would remain sturdy and safe for years to come.

A key element of the renovation was the installation of a wood-[[burn]]ing stove, with a 10-meter [[chimney]] designed to heat the space efficiently. old surfaces were restored, preserving the [[charm]] of the [[stone]] walls and the warmth of the old wooden floor. 

The project was executed with a minimalist approach, and the budget remained remarkably low, thanks to the use of reclaimed materials. Much of the wood came from the [[street]]s, salvaged and re[[purpose]]d, adding a layer of history and sustainability to the structure. This decision to use reused [[material]]s not only [[reduc]]ed costs but also tied the house to its [[surround]]ings, creating a deeper [[connect]]ion between the home and the [[environment]].
