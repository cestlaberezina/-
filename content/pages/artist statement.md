pauline pelagia garbbha (b. january 5, 1994, pskov, russia) is a somatic photographer and a grief doula. 

her thing is carrying the real through her body, photography, relational arts, previously clay and performance. pauline’s artistic ambition is to create spaces for people to resonate with the nature inside (their subjectivity) and outside (the real). she believes this pulsation between inner and outer worlds embraces the quiet potential of political action: a freedom for people to become toward a deeply ecological existence.

unrehearsed and intuitive, her work doesn’t aim to resolve but to catalyze — an adventure, a happening, a dialogue. shaped by theatre, philosophy, somatics and psychoanalysis, pauline blooms in the messy, liminal spaces where art and life collide.
