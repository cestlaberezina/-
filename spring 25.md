**In following the rhetoric of the dematerialization of the art object, the photograph became the only tangible record of these epiphenomena. Whether the artist was engaged in body art or earthworks or language games, the photograph provided the means to document conceptual art, and often became synonymous with the work of art itself in a collapsing of the photographic signifier with the signified event. But what must be recalled in the context of this final chapter is that the subject- matter of a number of these photographic documents was tinged with black humour and a deadpan delivery. These images acknowledge that all earthly bodies (whether animate or inanimate) have an intimate relationship with falling (and failing), but they also affirm the humour embedded in this sadly clownish state of affairs. As such, the conceptual pratfalls of these artists/ photographers acknowledge the levity of gravity.**
