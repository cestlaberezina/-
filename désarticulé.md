domaine de spécialité: artistic research, photography, site-specific performance
aim: to create photo essays on displacement
résumé: the artist will encounter spaces and places that are out of joint (displaced from their function, abandoned or places of cultural/ecological displacement, borderlands) and spend time with them alone or in public in order to create photo works and photo essays. the medium in this project is not photography, however, but rather site-specific performance for photo. core question is what is embodiment (body of an artist as a medium) of a space being once displaced, deterritorialized (guattari), deconstructed. a counterpoint to it is questioning what are attributes of displacement and the relation to visual language in between reverie (bauchelard) and action as gnosiologyical (epistemological) articulative modalities (aka how things differ if they are part of economics/function or not).

as i myself am displaced, i was always drawn to finding functional ways to connect to reality of others to survive, collecting attributes of normality (which is absolutely discoursive and in this sense political). in this project i shape the question differently: what is the place of displaced territories (that dont have obvious, socially approved and appropriate symbolization) in somatic perception and experience. as an artist, a woman in the world of machines, a person in exile, a neurodivergent individual i developed a strong tolerance to uncertainty and ambiguity; however, to exist in institutional forms as an artist is to be approved by the Other on the utter fact of existence of my work and the relevance of it (by curator, funding organisation, gallery). 

the method is rather archeological than constructive; i want to dust things away, not to build my research toward the statement or impression. i go out and i speculate; this project is  fundamentally experiential. 

the hypothesis is that the sentence might be built from an act of experiencing of the unspeakable (displaced, deterritorialized) in the shape of the photo essays.
